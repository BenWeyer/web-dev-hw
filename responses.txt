- Data doesn't necessarily travel in a fixed path from one destination to
another. Rather, packets of information travel in different paths depending
on internet traffic. Oftentimes the data users wish to send is too large for a
single packet so packets are split into smaller parts yet ultimately reach the
same destination.
- The Domain Name System (DNS) associates a website's name with the
corresponding IP Address.
- Cookies are small pieces of data that are sent from a website and stored in
your computer to ensure that website remembers who you are.
- The three basic parts of a URL are: protocol, domain name, and path.
