Traditional Ajiaco

Each region of Colombia has it own specialty dishes. For example, in the capital city, Bogotá, Ajiaco is a very popular dish.


There are different versions of Ajiaco Bogotano, but it’s usually made with chicken, three types of potatoes, corn, and an herb called guascas. This herb gives the soup a wonderful flavor. It is very important to use guascas and papa criolla as they are the key ingredients in this dish. For me, Ajiaco is a feel-good comfort food.

Buen provecho!

Ingredients

Makes 6-8 servings

  • 3 chicken breasts, skin removed
  • 12 cups water
  • 3 ears corn
  • 2 chicken bouillon cubes
  • 3 scallions
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 3 tablespoons chopped cilantro
  • 2 cups frozen papa criolla
  • 3 medium white potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 3 medium red potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 1/3 cup guascas
  • 1 cup heavy cream for serving
  • 1 cup capers for serving

Directions

  1. In a large pot, combine the chicken, corn, bouillon, cilantro, scallions, garlic, salt and pepper. Add the water and bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium and cook for about 35-40 minutes or until chicken is cooked and tender. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  2. Continue cooking the corn for 30 minutes. Discard green onion. Add red potatoes, white potatoes, and guacas. Cook for 30 minutes.
  3. Uncover and add the frozen papa criolla. Simmer for 15-20 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cut the chicken into small pieces and return to the pot. Serve the Ajiaco hot with capers and heavy cream on the side.
